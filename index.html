Strategy Deep Dive

AI First is a state of being, not an initiative. It is the goal — the cultural end-state where every employee, regardless of role, defaults to AI in their daily work. Enabled by AI is the parent initiative that houses all the actions, programmes, and workstreams driving the organisation toward that state. The training tracks are the "how." AI First is the "why" and the "where we're going."

What Is AI First?

Being AI First means AI is no longer something you "try out" or use occasionally. It becomes the natural starting point for how you work — the same way you instinctively open Excel for numbers or Outlook for email. You think AI before you think manual process.

Together, Enabled by AI is how we get to AI First.

The Five Behaviours of an AI First Employee

Across every role and department, an AI First employee consistently demonstrates these five behaviours:

1

Default to AI

When facing a new task, your instinct is to ask: "Can AI help me do this faster, better, or differently?" before starting manually. You reach for AI tools the way you reach for a calculator.

2

Spot AI opportunities

You naturally recognise patterns in your work that are ripe for AI: repetitive tasks, rule-based decisions, text-heavy processes (the 3 R's). You don't wait for someone to tell you where AI fits — you see it yourself.

3

Use AI tools confidently

Whether it's Falck GPT for quick questions and drafting, Copilot Studio for building agents, Claude Cowork for complex workflows, or GitHub Copilot for code — you know which tool fits which job, and you use them without hesitation.

4

Share and contribute

You don't keep your AI wins to yourself. You share prompts, use cases, and agents with colleagues. You contribute to the Use Case & Skills Library so others can build on your work.

5

Evaluate critically

You know AI isn't perfect. You verify outputs, catch hallucinations, understand limitations, and know when to trust, adjust, or override what AI gives you. You use AI responsibly and in line with Falck's guidelines.

What AI First Looks Like by Department

The behaviours are universal, but how they manifest depends on your role. Click to explore each department.

Finance & Accounting

▼
Before AI First
Manually consolidates monthly reports from multiple entities, cross-references figures in Excel, writes variance commentaries by hand. This takes days.
After AI First
Uses AI to draft variance commentaries from data extracts, generates first-pass consolidation summaries. The manual process that took 3 days now takes 1 — analyst time spent on judgment, not copy-paste.
Example AI First moments:
Using AI to draft month-end commentary from raw data before refining manually
Asking Falck GPT to explain a new IFRS standard in plain language
Building a Copilot Studio agent that answers recurring questions from budget owners

Legal

▼
Before AI First
Reads through entire contracts manually to find specific clauses, drafts NDAs from scratch each time, researches regulatory changes by reading newsletters line by line.
After AI First
Uses AI to scan contracts for key clauses and flag risks, generates first drafts of standard agreements. Time to first draft drops dramatically — expertise spent on negotiation and strategy.
Example AI First moments:
Using AI to extract and compare key terms across multiple vendor contracts
Having Falck GPT summarise a new regulation and identify what it means for Falck
Building a Legal AI Hub — a portfolio of agents handling routine legal queries

Procurement

▼
Before AI First
Manually reviews supplier proposals against requirements, writes evaluation summaries by hand, tracks compliance documentation across spreadsheets.
After AI First
Uses AI to compare proposals side-by-side, generate compliance scorecards, and draft supplier communication. Compliance Partner agent handles routine checks automatically.
Example AI First moments:
Using AI to draft RFP evaluation summaries from proposal documents
Building an agent that checks supplier documentation against compliance requirements
Asking Falck GPT to benchmark pricing against market data

M&A (Mergers & Acquisitions)

▼
Before AI First
Due diligence involves weeks of manual document review — financial statements, contracts, regulatory filings — analysts reading hundreds of pages to identify risks.
After AI First
AI-Powered Due Diligence agents process document sets in hours, extracting key financial metrics, flagging risk clauses, generating structured summaries. Team focuses on strategic assessment.
Example AI First moments:
Using AI to extract and structure key financials from target company documents
Having an agent scan all contracts in a data room for change-of-control clauses
Using Falck GPT to research market context for a potential acquisition target

After AI First
Uses AI to generate first drafts of job descriptions, summarise candidate profiles, draft policy updates, and build an agent that answers routine HR questions from the employee handbook.
Example AI First moments:
Using AI to draft a job posting and tailor it for different channels
Building a Copilot Studio agent that answers common HR policy questions
Using Falck GPT to summarise employee survey results and identify themes

Marketing & Communications

▼
Before AI First
Manually drafts content for multiple channels, resizes and adapts messaging for different audiences, spends hours on first drafts of articles, social posts, and internal communications.
After AI First
AI generates first drafts across channels — social posts, articles, internal newsletters — which the team refines for brand voice. Content production doubles in speed while maintaining quality.

Using AI to generate 5 social post variations from a single press release

Using AI to draft an internal newsletter summarising the quarter's achievements

Data Insights & AI — The Proving Ground

This department reaches AI First faster than any other because it has the mandate, the tools, and the culture to experiment first. What works here gets validated and scaled to the broader CFO organisation.

AI Fridays — Department-wide sessions where the team explores tools, shares discoveries, and practices new techniques together
Use Case & Skills Library — Shared repository of reusable prompts, agents, and skills (hosted as downloadable MD files in a DevOps repo)
Tool Onboarding — Non-developers learning VS Code, Git, and GitHub to work with AI development tools
Structured Interviews — Systematic one-on-one conversations mapping workflows and identifying highest-impact AI opportunities
Team-level deep dives — Tailored AI integration support for each team within the department
Every practice above is designed to be repeatable. When AI Fridays work here, the format can be adopted by Finance. When the Skills Library proves valuable, it can be opened to all of CFO. The Data department is the R&D lab for AI First culture.

Measuring AI First

AI First is a cultural shift, so measurement is about behaviours, not just tool usage.

Leading indicators (early signs of progress)
Number of employees who have completed at least one training track
Falck GPT monthly active users (currently 2.8K)
Number of use cases submitted to the Skills Library
Attendance at AI Fridays and similar practice sessions
Lagging indicators (evidence of cultural shift)
Percentage of employees who report using AI weekly in their work
Number of departments running their own AI First practices
Time-to-completion improvements on key business processes
Number of AI agents/solutions in active production use
The ultimate measure: When a new employee joins Falck and asks "how do I do this?" — if the answer naturally includes "here's the AI tool we use for that" — we've reached AI First.
